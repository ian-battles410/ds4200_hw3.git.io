Energy Price Prediction Models

Introduction | Models | Contact

All models were trained on 2023 data and tested on 2024 data.

  • Nonlinear Regression (Massachusetts dataset)

    • This model predicts the nonlinear relationship between temperature and energy price. While effective at modeling trends, it does not fully capture price spikes.

  • Linear Model (California dataset)

    • This model predicts how solar radiation affects energy prices but does not fully account for variance at lower solar levels.

  • Monte Carlo Simulation (California dataset)

    • The Monte Carlo simulation effectively predicts yearly energy prices based on solar radiation. It is useful for forecasting long-term trends rather than short-term fluctuations.
